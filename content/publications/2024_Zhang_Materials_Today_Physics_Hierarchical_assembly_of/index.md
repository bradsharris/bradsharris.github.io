abstract: "Bio-macromolecular assembly forms intricate scaffolds in living systems that allow them to biomineralize bone, enamel, and shell. Such bioinspired approaches have also been widely used in biohybrid materials synthesis. Recently, researchers have demonstrated functional bioelectronic structures by assembling biopolymers, including proteins and peptides, on van der Waals (VdW) materials for sensors and energy storage and harvesting. However, the hierarchical assembly of biopolymers on VdW materials has yet to be fully documented, because modulating the assembly architectures by controlling the energy landscape along with environmental stimuli, in which various assembly phases occur, remains challenging.
