abstract: "Molecular dynamics (MD) simulations in the MARTINI model are used to study the assembly of 1-palmitoyl-2-oleoylphosphatidylcholine (POPC) molecules under spatial confinement, such as during solvent evaporation from ultrasmall (femtoliter quantity) droplets. The impact of surface polarity on molecular assembly is discussed in detail. To the best of our knowledge, this work represents the first of its kind. Our results reveal that solvent evaporation gives rise to the formation of well-defined stacks of lipid bilayers in a smectic alignment. These smectic mesophases form on both polar and nonpolar surfaces but with a notable distinction. On polar surfaces, the director of the stack is oriented perpendicular to the support surface.
