abstract: "SARS‐CoV‐2 Spike is a key protein that mediates viral entry into cells and elicits antibody responses. Its importance in infection, diagnostics, and vaccinations has created a large demand for purified Spike for clinical and research applications. Spike is difficult to express, prompting modifications to the protein and expression platforms to improve yields. Alternatively, the Spike receptor‐binding domain (RBD) is commonly expressed with higher titers, though it has lower sensitivity in serological assays. Here, we improve transient Spike expression in Chinese hamster ovary (CHO) cells. We demonstrate that Spike titers increase significantly over the expression period, maximizing at 14 mg L−1 on day 7. In comparison, RBD titers peak at 54 mg L−1 on day 3. Next, we develop eight Spike truncations (T1–T8) in pursuit of truncation with high expression and antibody binding.
